According to research from the National Center of Biotechnology Information, painting enables the ability to think in a different manner, encouraging open-ended thought and creativity. In addition to creativity. There are cognitive and cerebral benefits to painting. We use our brains when we paint or draw, and this not only releases endorphins, but helps build new connections and pathways. When painting, we actively use both sides of our brain, the right for creativity, and the left for logical thinking. This strengthens both and helps develop the ability to focus and think strategically. Painting, like many other art forms, aids in relaxation and stress relief by forcing us to pay attention to details in the environment, which mimics the experience of mediation. painting is also said to relieve stress and improve focus, as it relieves your brain from the strain of continuous concentration. This, in turn, allows you to relax.
